As a GRC analyst, I am interested in the place where compliance frameworks and human judgment intersect: the moment when someone decides whether a control is worth maintaining, whether a deadline matters, whether a vendor's word is good enough. That is rarely a technical decision. It is a cultural and governance one. My background in counseling psychology shapes how I think about that intersection hardly as a soft counterweight to hard technical facts, but as the layer of analysis that explains why technically correct remediation plans fail in practice. These cases I investigate and write about are really not unusual, and that is the point.
